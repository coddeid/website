Balinese Culture

NYEPI Balinese New Year

Nyepi is known ad the Day of Silence. You should know, during Nyepi is a sacred Hindu holiday in Bali, and an imprtant public holiday for the rest of Indonesia.

Traditional Bali Dance

Most Balinese dances are inspired by the Hindu epic of Ramayana and Mahabarata. The traditon of these forms and movement young age. They are required to train with under precise rules and dicipline schedule. Most types of dances will require vivacious and gracious movement of all parts of the body, including expressive facial, eyes, and hand gestures.

Legong Dance

Legong Dance is concidered Bali's most graceful dance, combining detailed finger movement, complicated steps and expressive eye movements. The classical dance is olnly performed by young female dancers often as young as 8 or 9 years old.

The Nine Directional Temple

Unlike other temples, wich only allow people from a spesific family lineage with very strict customs regulations. These nine temples are open and functions as pilgrimage destinations for all Hindu-Bali worshipper in the island. Unfortunately, it's stictly forbidden for tourist to enter the worship area, both domestic and foreign.

Gamelan

Balinese Gamelan refers to the orchestra arrangement that are made up of predomunantly percussive instrument. The traditional ensemble music is most widely performed in Javanese, Sundanese, and Balinese, and is considered a significant representation of Indonesian culture.

Tooth Feeling Ceremony

in Bali, teeth filing is a cultural procedure used to reduce 'savage' traits such as greed, desire, and rage, wich they refer to as "Sad Ripu". This ritual is known as metatah or mepandes, wich means "to chisel/carve.

Balinese Kite

"A Child-like but Traditional Activity in the Dry Season".Bali's usual clear blue skies will be decorated with colorful kites flown by joyful kids and grown-ups all over the island. Sometime beetween June until September are considered as the best period to play this traditional particular game as Indonesia as the wind during the dry season is strong enough to get the kites reaching unbelievable height in the sky.

Balinese Art

Balinese art is art of Hindu-Javanese origin that grew from the work of artisans of the Majapahit Kingdom, with their expansion to Bali in the late 14 century.
